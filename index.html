Simple past tense


Simple past tense:
We use the simple past tense to talk about something that happened and was finished in the past.

ðŸ‘‰Rule: Subject + Verb in the past form (base form of the verb + ed/d for regular verbs or past tense form of irregular verbs)

For regular verbs, add "-ed" to the end of the verb (like "play" becomes "played"). For irregular verbs, the word changes in its own way (like "go" becomes "went").

Examples:
"I played with my toys yesterday."
"She ate ice cream last night."
