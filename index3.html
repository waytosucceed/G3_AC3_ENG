Past continuous tense


Past Continuous Tense:
We use the past continuous tense to talk about something that was happening in the past for a period of time. It shows that the action was ongoing.

ðŸ‘‰ Rule: Subject + was/were + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence.
We use "was" for I/he/she/it and "were" for you/we/they.

Examples:
"I was playing outside when it started to rain."
"She was reading a book while her brother was drawing."
"They were playing in the park."

We use the simple past tense for actions that started and ended in the past, and we use the past continuous tense to describe actions that were happening for a while in the past.
